01
What Melatonin Actually Is — Beyond the Sleep Label
Hardeland 2012 · Acuña-Castroviejo et al., Cell Mol Life Sci 2014 · Reiter RJ et al.

Melatonin (N-acetyl-5-methoxytryptamine) is produced primarily by the pineal gland under control of the hypothalamic suprachiasmatic nucleus. Production rises steeply after dark, peaks at 2–4 AM, and falls before dawn. But the "sleep hormone" label dramatically undersells its biology.

Pineal vs. Extrapineal Production
Acuña-Castroviejo et al., Cell Mol Life Sci 2014

The pineal gland produces circulating melatonin for systemic signaling. But melatonin is also produced locally in the gut, ovaries, skin, bone marrow, and immune cells — often at concentrations many times higher than plasma levels. This extrapineal production is paracrine in function. The gut may produce more melatonin than the pineal gland in total mass.

Receptor Distribution Explains Everything
MT1 · MT2 · MT3 (QR2) receptor system

MT1 and MT2 receptors are found not just in the brain's circadian centers but throughout: ovaries, uterus, granulosa cells, cardiovascular tissue, adipose tissue, gut, and immune cells. MT3 is a cytosolic enzyme (QR2) that scavenges reactive oxygen species directly. This receptor distribution explains why melatonin biology extends so far beyond sleep.

1
Circadian Signal
Primary function: biochemical signal of darkness. Regulates sleep-wake architecture, circadian rhythm, and seasonal biology. Production declines with age — significantly so after menopause.
2
Antioxidant — Tier 1
Directly scavenges free radicals and reactive oxygen species (ROS) via the MT3/QR2 pathway. Activates endogenous antioxidant enzymes: superoxide dismutase (SOD), catalase, glutathione peroxidase, and glutathione reductase. Maintains cellular redox homeostasis across all tissues.
3
Anti-Inflammatory — NF-κB Pathway
Prevents translocation of NF-κB to the nucleus, blocking transcription of pro-inflammatory cytokines including IL-1, IL-6, and TNF-α. Also downregulates 5-lipoxygenase, a key inflammation activator. This makes melatonin directly relevant to any chronic inflammatory condition.
4
Immunomodulatory + Metabolic
Regulates immune cell function, enhances insulin sensitivity via PI3K/AKT signaling, modulates brown adipose thermogenesis, influences leptin and ghrelin, and synchronizes peripheral cellular clocks throughout the body.
5
Reproductive Hormone Modulator
Acts directly on ovarian granulosa cells (which express MT1 and MT2). Stimulates progesterone production from luteal cells, regulates LH pulsatility, protects oocytes from oxidative damage, and modulates the hypothalamic-pituitary-gonadal axis. Women with PCOS have measurably lower melatonin concentrations in follicular fluid.

The Menstrual Cycle Connection — Melatonin Is Hormonally Integrated
Bauer et al., J Endocr Soc 2020 (SWAN DHS · n=20) · Cagnacci et al. 1995 · ScienceDirect 2007

Melatonin isn't hormonally neutral across the female cycle. Research from the Study of Women's Health Across the Nation has documented a natural rise in melatonin during the late luteal phase — the two weeks after ovulation. This has direct implications for how and when to supplement.

Melatonin Across the Menstrual Cycle — Documented Luteal Rise
Bauer et al., J Endocr Soc 2020 · Wetterberg et al. 1976 (4.5× luteal elevation, original finding)
FOLLICULAR PHASE OVULATION LUTEAL PHASE ↑ MELATONIN

Key Mechanism The strongest predictor of melatonin levels in the late luteal phase is progesterone (PdG) values 11–12 days prior (β = 1.46, p = .001). Melatonin directly stimulates progesterone production from luteal granulosa cells — creating a bidirectional relationship. This is why supplementing melatonin during the luteal phase (days 14–28) aligns with the body's natural rhythm rather than disrupting it. After menopause, the luteal melatonin rise disappears entirely — coinciding with the hormonal disruption of the menopausal transition.
Practical Implication — Luteal Phase Cycling
Some practitioners recommend taking melatonin specifically during the luteal phase (days 14–28 of the cycle) rather than year-round. This mimics and supports the body's own progesterone-triggered melatonin rise, may help with PMS/PMDD sleep disruption and mood symptoms during that phase, and avoids any potential downregulation of the natural rise during the follicular phase. Cycling (5 days on/2 off, or luteal-only) is a reasonable approach with biological rationale — though long-term daily use in clinical trials has not shown dependency.

PCOS — Women Are Melatonin-Deficient in Their Follicles
Ziaei et al., J Ovarian Res 2024 (6 RCTs meta-analysis) · Patel et al., Cureus 2023 · Li et al., BMC Womens Health 2022

Polycystic ovary syndrome affects ~10% of women of reproductive age. Research has confirmed that women with PCOS have measurably lower melatonin concentrations in their follicular fluid compared to women without the condition. Oxidative stress is a core driver of PCOS pathology — which makes melatonin's antioxidant properties directly mechanistically relevant.

Important Caveat The 2024 meta-analysis of 6 PCOS RCTs (Ziaei et al.) confirmed antioxidant benefits but found no statistically significant effect on fasting blood sugar, HOMA-IR, cholesterol fractions, CRP, androgens, SHBG, or pregnancy rate in the pooled analysis. The antioxidant and anti-inflammatory benefits are well-supported; the metabolic and hormonal benefits are biologically plausible and seen in individual trials but not yet confirmed in meta-analysis. PCOS is a heterogeneous condition and individual response likely varies significantly.

Mechanism — Follicular Antioxidant
ROS↓
Melatonin is naturally concentrated in follicular fluid. It scavenges ROS that damage oocyte mitochondria, reduces apoptosis in granulosa cells, and improves mitochondrial function — the core drivers of oocyte quality.
The Day-3 Embryo Finding
Independent in vitro studies with human embryos demonstrated that melatonin improves the quality of human day-3 embryos specifically in patients with a history of poor embryo development — with reported increases in fertilization, cleavage, high-quality blastocyst development, implantation, and clinical pregnancy rates. Myo-inositol + melatonin combinations show additive benefit in IVF patients with PCOS and poor oocyte quality history. Typical protocol in trials: 2–6 mg nightly, starting 6–8 weeks before retrieval.

Perimenopause, Menopause & PMS — Melatonin Declines With Ovarian Aging
Garzon et al., Maturitas 2006 · Bauer et al. 2020 · He et al., Front Psychiatry 2023 · Demirhan 2024 meta-analysis

Melatonin declines measurably from premenopause to postmenopause — and this decline likely contributes to the disrupted sleep, mood instability, and hormonal dysregulation that characterize the menopausal transition. The loss of the luteal melatonin rise with ovarian aging removes a key circadian synchronizer.

The Dose Problem — Why Most People Take 10× Too Much
Zhdanova et al., J Clin Endocrinol Metab 2001 · MIT · Cruz-Sanabria, J Pineal Res 2024 (dose-response meta-analysis)

This is the single most important practical takeaway from the melatonin research — and it directly contradicts what you'll find on most supplement shelves. The standard dose sold in the US is 5–10 mg. The evidence says this is pharmacologically excessive for sleep use in most people.

Why Supraphysiological Doses Backfire
The 3 mg dose caused plasma melatonin to remain elevated into daylight hours and induced mild hypothermia — without improving sleep above the 0.3 mg result. At 10 mg, levels would reach 10–16× above normal nocturnal concentrations, staying elevated well into the morning. This explains the grogginess ("melatonin hangover") people often blame on the supplement itself — it's a dose problem, not a melatonin problem. At supraphysiological doses, there is theoretical concern for receptor desensitization and suppression of natural feedback. Use the lowest effective dose.

Debunking the Anti-Melatonin Headlines — Correlation ≠ Causation
National Geographic Dec 2025 · Rao NYU Langone commentary · Observational data critique

You'll occasionally see headlines warning that melatonin causes heart problems, dependency, or long-term suppression. These claims almost universally stem from observational data — and the causal arrow is almost certainly reversed.

The "Melatonin Causes Heart Risk" Claim — Anatomy of a Misleading Headline
Observational data vs. mechanistic + RCT literature · Rao, NYU Langone commentary
!
The Observational Finding
People who take melatonin supplements → have higher rates of cardiovascular events in some observational studies. Headlines: "Melatonin Raises Heart Risk."
→
The Obvious Confound
People with poor sleep → take melatonin. People with poor sleep → also have higher cardiovascular risk. People with heart disease → often have poor sleep → take melatonin. The association is real. The causal arrow is backwards. Melatonin didn't cause their heart disease — their heart disease (and poor sleep) prompted melatonin use.
✓
What Controlled Evidence Shows
The RCT and mechanistic literature tells the opposite story: melatonin reduces nocturnal hypertension, improves endothelial function, decreases platelet aggregation, lowers LDL, reduces CRP, and attenuates cardiac ischemia-reperfusion injury. The 63-RCT meta-analysis showed significant SBP, LDL, and fasting glucose reductions. The cardioprotective mechanisms are well-established.
The Stanford Cardiologist Quote Problem
A cardiologist at Stanford was quoted saying she doesn't recommend melatonin because of "observational studies showing an association with heart failure." This is the epidemiological equivalent of saying "ambulances cause car accidents because they're always at crash scenes." The association does not establish causation. The people who needed melatonin were sicker to begin with. When the same question is examined with controlled trials — the only methodology that can establish causation — melatonin shows cardioprotective effects.
Myth "Melatonin is addictive / causes dependency"

Multiple long-term trials: no evidence of physical dependency, tolerance, or rebound insomnia upon discontinuation. Does not suppress REM sleep. Mechanistically distinct from benzodiazepines/Z-drugs.
Myth "It permanently shuts down your own production"

No convincing human evidence for permanent suppression at therapeutic doses. Short-term feedback modulation at supraphysiological doses is plausible — another reason to use the lowest effective dose (0.3 mg for sleep).
Myth "The IVF benefits are just placebo"

You cannot placebo your way to a 59% improvement in clinical pregnancy rates across 11 RCTs with near-zero heterogeneity (I²=0%) — a finding that reflects improved oocyte quality and fertilization rates in blinded controlled trials.

Regulatory Context — US vs. Europe
In the United States, melatonin is classified as a dietary supplement — no prescription required, widely available at 1–10 mg doses (often too high). In the UK, EU, and Australia, melatonin is prescription-only — typically prescribed at 2 mg controlled-release. This discrepancy means American consumers routinely self-dose at pharmacological levels without clinical oversight, while European users get appropriate dosing under medical guidance. The European model is arguably more appropriate given the dose-response data.
Synthesis · The Complete Evidence Picture
Melatonin is dramatically undercharacterized as a "sleep supplement." It is a pleiotropic hormone with documented effects across reproductive health, cardiovascular protection, metabolic regulation, inflammation, and neuroprotection — operating through a well-characterized receptor system expressed throughout the body.

For women specifically, the evidence is uniquely compelling. The menstrual cycle is biologically integrated with melatonin production — progesterone drives a late-luteal melatonin rise that disappears after menopause. Women with PCOS are melatonin-deficient in their follicular fluid. In fertility and IVF, a 2025 meta-analysis of 11 RCTs (1,481 women) found a 59% increase in odds of clinical pregnancy — with near-zero heterogeneity, driven by improved oocyte quality, fertilization rates, and embryo development. For perimenopausal women, clinical trial data shows thyroid function improvement, gonadotropin normalization, and abrogation of menopausal depression after 6 months of 3 mg nightly.

On dosing: The Zhdanova 2001 MIT study demonstrated that 0.3 mg outperforms 3 mg and 10 mg on objective polysomnographic sleep measures. The standard 5–10 mg US supplement dose produces plasma melatonin 10–16× above normal nocturnal levels, causes daytime elevation, and adds grogginess without clinical benefit for sleep. Use the lowest effective dose. The therapeutic doses used in PCOS, IVF, and anti-inflammatory trials (2–6 mg) are appropriate for those clinical applications — with clinician oversight.

On the negative headlines: The observational associations between melatonin use and cardiovascular events are textbook confounding — sick people with poor sleep use melatonin. The controlled trial evidence runs in the opposite direction: cardioprotective. The safety profile is among the cleanest in supplementation — no dependency, no REM suppression, no serious adverse events across RCTs in diverse populations.
