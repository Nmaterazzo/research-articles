Postprandial glucose "spikes" in healthy people are adaptive physiology, not pathology. The true metabolic threat is chronic energy surplus → lipotoxicity. Obsessing over acute excursions is a red herring.

What the evidence actually shows about blood glucose, insulin resistance, and why CGMs mislead healthy people.

1

Normal PPG peaks of 120–139 mg/dL sit well below the 140 mg/dL IGT threshold. ADA/WHO define normal glucose tolerance as <140 mg/dL at 2 hours on the OGTT — healthy postprandial spikes should stay inside that range.

2

Insulin resistance is caused by DAG and ceramide lipid intermediates from chronic caloric surplus — not glucose speed. PKC-θ activation blocks IRS-1 signaling. The structural failure is lipid-driven.

3

CGMs in non-diabetics systematically overestimate glucose by ~15 mg/dL and overstate time-above-threshold by ~27%. They misclassify whole fruits as high-GI.

The U-CurveA systematic review and meta-analysis confirmed a U-shaped association between HbA1c and all-cause mortality in non-diabetics. Optimal survival range: 5.0–6.0%. Risk increases significantly both below 5.0% AND above 6.0%. Both extremes carry elevated mortality.
Low HbA1c RiskChronically low HbA1c (<5.0%) often reflects poor nutritional status, frailty, or serious non-metabolic disease — not optimal glucose control. This directly contradicts aggressive glucose minimization as a health strategy.
Key InsightAchieving the optimal 5.0–6.0% range requires robust glucose flux — meaning the system must efficiently process dietary carbohydrates including their transient postprandial excursions. You cannot maintain a healthy HbA1c while avoiding all carbohydrate processing.
Metabolic Resilience = Efficient Clearance, Not Spike Avoidance
A healthy metabolic system is defined by rapid return to baseline after a glucose load — not by minimizing the peak itself. High insulin sensitivity means the system clears glucose efficiently. The peak is the signal; clearance speed is the health marker.

The Real Mechanism — IR Is Lipid-Driven, Not Glucose-Driven

How Insulin Resistance Actually Develops

Step 1 — Chronic Surplus
Adipose storage capacity exceeded → adipocyte hypertrophy → ectopic fat deposits in liver, muscle, pancreas
↓
Step 2 — Lipotoxicity
Triglyceride overflow → DAG and ceramide generation in non-adipose tissue — driven by fatty acid flux, not glucose speed
↓
Step 3 — PKC-θ Activation
DAG activates Protein Kinase C-theta → serine phosphorylation of IRS-1 at Ser312 → blocks tyrosine phosphorylation cascade
↓
Step 4 — IR Established
GLUT4 translocation impaired → glucose clearance fails → T2D pathway. A structural signaling failure — not a glucose-speed problem.
GI/GL Null ResultA systematic review and meta-analysis of randomized clinical trials found no significant effect of dietary GI or GL on hs-CRP, leptin, IL-6, or TNF-α — the chronic inflammatory markers that actually drive insulin resistance. (Milajerdi et al., 2018 · Am J Clin Nutr)
Why High-Fiber Diets WorkWhere high-fiber, low-GI foods DO help — it's through satiety and reduced total caloric intake, not through blunting the acute spike. The benefit is caloric management. GI/GL manipulation is largely a nutritional red herring for IR prevention.
The Primary Target
Preventing chronic caloric surplus is the primary therapeutic lever for IR prevention. This prevents adipose overflow, DAG/ceramide accumulation, PKC-θ activation, and IRS-1 blockade. Targeting the acute glucose spike addresses a downstream symptom while the upstream structural cause goes untreated.

Context-Dependent Vascular RiskPostprandial glucose elevation is an independent CVD risk factor primarily in Type 2 Diabetics with impaired clearance — not in insulin-sensitive individuals. Serious complications arise when hyperglycemia persists, not from transient physiological excursions. Metabolic memory studies are predominantly in diabetic cell cultures, not healthy humans.
The Core Argument
When diagnostic criteria define values below 140 mg/dL as normal, focusing anxiously on postprandial excursions that peak at 130 mg/dL is alarmist. The optimal goal for a healthy person is rapid return to baseline — demonstrating high insulin sensitivity — not maximum suppression of the postprandial peak.

The device designed to help diabetics actively misguides healthy people.
For Non-Diabetics: CGMs Are Not Necessary
CGMs are designed for metabolic conditions. For healthy individuals, the gold standard metric remains HbA1c maintained at 5.0–6.0% — a validated chronic marker reflecting integrated glucose handling over 2–3 months, not acute device noise from physiological non-events.
Synthesis · The Strategic Reframe
Transient postprandial glucose excursions in non-diabetic individuals are adaptive physiological signals — not pathological events. The healthy metabolic system handles them efficiently. The structural failure that leads to insulin resistance is caused by chronic caloric surplus → ectopic lipid deposition → DAG/ceramide-mediated PKC-θ activation → IRS-1 blockade. That pathway is entirely decoupled from carbohydrate absorption speed.

A healthy HbA1c of 5.0–6.0% requires robust glucose flux — meaning the system must process dietary carbohydrates including their transient excursions. Metabolic resilience is defined by clearance efficiency, not spike elimination.
